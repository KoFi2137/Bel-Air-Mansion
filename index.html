About Bel Air

Bel Air position

The borough is approximately 19 km (12 miles) west of downtown Los Angeles in the Westside Hills of the foothills of the Santa Monica Mountains. On the south side, via Sunset Boulevard, it is adjacent to the University of California Los Angeles (UCLA). Together with Beverly Hills and Holmby Hills, she forms the Platinum Triangle of Los Angeles neighborhoods.

Building

Bel Air properties are among the most expensive in the United States. The residences are well guarded and away from the winding roads here. Most of it is not visible from the street, it is surrounded by gates, fences and manicured hedges. Multi-family housing is not allowed, strict building and architectural regulations apply in the district. The style of the residence ranges from modest farm style to multi-story mansions. Bel Air is characterized by lots of greenery. The exclusive Bel Air Country Club and Bel-Air Hotel are located in the heart of the district. Unlike Beverly Hills, Bel Air has no sidewalks, which is intended to discourage passers-by from walking in the area.

Curiosities

  1. The action of the popular comedy series Bajer from Bel-Air (Fresh Prince of Bel Air) was set in this district.
  2. The name of the district is derived from the name of the Chevrolet Bel Air produced in 1953-1978 by General Motors.
  3. The district is mentioned by thrash metal band Exodus in their song Burn, Hollywood, Burn (album Exhibit B: The Human Condition).
  4. The Quarter is the title of a song by author Lana Del Rey, where she compares this place to heaven.
